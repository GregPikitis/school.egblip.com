Boyle's Law
Pictured is a graph representing the relationship between the pressure and volume of gas. As the pressure gets increases, the volume decreases as seen in the bottom right of the graph, and vice versa in the top left.

Boyle's law relates pressure and volume while temperature remains constant. The law states that at a constant temperature, the pressure and volume of a gas are inversely proportional. Specifically, doubling the pressure of a gas, decreases the volume by one half.
Formula: PiVi = PfVf
i = initial V/P
f = final V/P
