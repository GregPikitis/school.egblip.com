Real World Example 2
How does a football shrink when inflated inside and taken outside in the cold?
The football is pumped full of air while in a warm room, and taken outdoors in a cold climate.

A football shrinks when it gets cold because the air insides’ temperature is decreasing, causing the volume of the air inside to also decrease, making the ball shrink. This is confirmed by the Charles’s Gas Law. As the temperature of a gas decreases, as does the volume. For more information, see Charles's Law.
